Frequently Asked

What is coaching, and how is it different from mentorship?

Coaching is a sustained relational practice built around your own definition of achievement — a continuous working relationship with targeted feedback and a framework for action that's entirely yours. A mentor shares the path they walked; coaching helps you find and walk your own.

I'm a senior designer — shouldn't I have this figured out by now?

No. Seniority means you've developed real craft and navigated real complexity — it doesn't mean the deeper questions of creative identity and meaning resolve themselves. Most of the designers I work with are highly capable people who have simply never had the space to examine what they actually want from their work.

What does a coaching session actually look like?

Each session is a co-processing conversation — we work through what's live for you, examine what's underneath it, and close with a set of actions to explore before we meet again. Structured but not prescriptive. The direction always comes from you.

What is creative invigoration, and how is it different from recovering from burnout?

Burnout recovery is about getting back to baseline — easing the immediate depletion. Creative invigoration is a different aim: the return of a genuine, self-authored aliveness — the kind that doesn't depend on your job to supply it, and that your work gets to draw from once it's there.

How does AI fit into your coaching practice?

The anxiety most designers feel about AI right now is real — and it's also one of the most clarifying entry points into understanding your own creative value. My coaching helps clients build a healthy, generative relationship with AI tools that enhances their thinking rather than replacing it.

Who do you coach?

Designers and design leaders at the intermediate through leadership level — those established in the industry looking to grow within their circumstances. I work primarily with UX designers and researchers, and also with creatives in adjacent fields. Single-session consultations are available for those earlier in their career.

How long does a coaching engagement typically last?

It varies — and that's intentional. Some clients come for a focused three-month engagement around a specific transition. Others work with me on an ongoing basis as their practice evolves. We start with a complimentary conversation, and if it's a fit, I'll propose a structure that reflects where you are.

Are you a motivational coach?

No. My practice is grounded in self-determination theory — working with your existing motivation and building sustainable psychological foundations beneath it. The goal is self-motivation that doesn't require someone standing over you to maintain.

Will you help me find a job or get a promotion?

That's not the focus of this practice. Some coaches specialize in placement and title — the resume, the portfolio, the interview. My work sits in a different place: the creative identity and values that make a career meaningful. Clearer direction and better opportunities tend to follow from that, as a natural result rather than the starting point.

What if we don't work well together?

The complimentary first conversation exists precisely to figure that out. No pressure, no obligation. If it doesn't feel like the right fit, I'll say so honestly — and I'm happy to point you toward coaches I know and trust so you're not left without options.
