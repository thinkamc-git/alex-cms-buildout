What comes after the UI Technician

AI didn't break UX — it stripped away the methods that hid what the discipline was always supposed to be. The reset is a return to thinking, not a story about new tools.

Principle
Mar 4, 2026 Updated May 2, 2026
AC
Alex M. Chong UX Design Director & Career Coach
14 min read

UX 1.0 was a discipline built around scarcity — scarce access to users, scarce ability to prototype, scarce tools to test. AI dissolved the scarcity entirely.

For two decades, UX justified its existence through its methods. The research process, the prototype handoff, the usability study — these were the discipline. They were hard to do without a specialist, which gave the role its shape.

"The gap between strategist and executor sits openly in the room now."

UX 2.0 is the return of foundational design thinking as the core competency — well beyond a story about new tools.

I keep returning to the question of who the work is for, even when nobody asks.

Apr 18, 2026
AC
Alex M. Chong UX Design Director & Career Coach

The honest answer is: most days, the work is for me. To process. To metabolise. To find a thread back to the part of myself that started this practice in the first place.

Other days the work is for an audience I can't quite picture — someone, somewhere, who needs the same problem framed in the way I'm trying to frame it. I write to them. They never write back.

And occasionally, the work is for the future of the discipline. Those are the rarest and longest entries.

Designing in the Age of Abundance

A 60-minute talk on what changes when designers no longer compete on artefact production.

Posted Apr 12, 2026
When

Where

Free In-person Talk · 60 min
AC

The post-AI design conversation has been preoccupied with the wrong question. We keep asking: which tools survive? The interesting question is: which skills become valuable when the tools no longer differentiate?

This talk frames three shifts already underway in serious teams: editorial judgement over execution velocity, intent over interface, and strategic clarity treated as a deliverable.
